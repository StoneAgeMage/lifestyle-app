Lentil Loaf

Recipe Book: Rowing Meal Plan Book
Servings: 10 servings
Active Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 1 hour 20 minutes

Ingredients

- 16 oz. white mushrooms
- 2 large carrots
- 2 Tbsp sun dried tomatoes in oil (processed very fine)
- 1 Tbsp olive oil
- ½ medium onion (diced very small)
- 1½ tsp sea salt
- 1 tsp black pepper (freshly cracked)
- 3 cups cooked lentils (divided)
- 4 garlic cloves (minced)
- ½ cups walnuts (processed)
- 2 Tbsp ground flaxseed
- 2 Tbsp ketchup
- ¼ cup breadcrumbs
- 1 Tbsp soy sauce
- 2 Tbsp fresh parsley (minced)
- ½ cup ketchup
- 1 Tbsp brown sugar
- 1 Tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard

Directions

1. Gather ingredients and preheat oven to 350℉.

2. Chop

3. Roughly chop mushrooms, carrots, and sun dried tomatoes to give your food processor a little extra help and ensure that the fresh ingredients are about the same size once processed.

4. Process carrots

5. First, process the carrots until they are shredded, with no pieces larger than ¼ inch.

6. Process mushrooms

7. Set aside processed carrots and add the mushrooms to the empty food processor. Pulse until crumbly and roughly the same size as the carrots.

8. Sauté

9. Add 1 Tbsp olive oil (or sun dried tomato oil, if you have it!) to a sauté pan over medium heat. Add the mushrooms, carrots, ½ a finely diced onion, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine and cook over medium heat while you prepare the rest of the ingredients, stirring occasionally.

10. Process

11. In the same food processor, add half the cooked lentils, garlic, sun dried tomatoes, and walnuts. Process to combine.

12. Simmer

13. You will notice the sauté pan is starting to look pretty juicy! The mushrooms have begun to release water, creating the perfect amount of moisture to keep your lentil loaf nice and juicy.

14. Stir

15. Add the rest of the whole lentils, ground flaxmeal, 2 Tbsp ketchup, breadcrumbs, and 1 Tbsp soy sauce. Stir to combine.

16. Combine

17. Next, add the sun dried tomato lentil mixture from the food processor and cook, stirring often, for another 10-15 minutes over medium heat. The mixture will soak up all of the juices from the mushrooms, and the flax will begin to help bind the lentils and veggies together, creating a dough-like paste.

18. Mix

19. Remove the cooked lentil mixture from the heat and allow it to cool for 10 or so minutes so it’s easy to handle. Meanwhile, mix the ½ cup ketchup, brown sugar, 1 Tbsp soy sauce, and Dijon mustard together and set aside.

20. Make the loaf

21. Once the lentil mixture has cooled, form it into a loaf on a parchment-lined baking sheet. My loaf was roughly 9.5” long, 6” wide, 1.5” tall. This is a hearty meal, and it feeds a crowd!

22. Add glaze

23. Using a spoon or an elevated spatula, cover the lentil loaf with the prepared sauce, being careful to lightly cover the sides, but taking special care to make sure you have no bare spots on top.

24. Bake for 45 minutes. Remove from the oven and top with minced parsley. Let it cool for 10 minutes or so before slicing and serving!
